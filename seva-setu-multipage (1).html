Eligibility

Who Can Apply?

Seva Setu is open to all legally registered Non-Profit Organizations in India.

🏛️

Trusts

Registered under the Indian Trusts Act or state-level trust legislation with a valid trust deed and current trustees.

🤝

Societies

Registered under the Societies Registration Act, 1860 with a functioning governing body and current bylaws.

🏢

Section 8 Companies

Non-profit companies incorporated under Section 8 of the Companies Act, 2013 with valid CIN.

Registration Process

How to Register for SETU Unique ID

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about registration, eligibility, and the Setu ID.

What is a Setu Unique ID?
A Setu Unique ID is a digital identifier assigned to verified Non-Profit Organizations to enable access to government grant portals and schemes. It is issued on the basis of a self-declared affidavit and does not constitute a government endorsement.
Is registration on Seva Setu mandatory?
While not legally mandatory, a Setu ID may be required by specific ministries before processing grant applications. Check the eligibility criteria of each grant portal before applying.
Does the Setu ID serve as a government endorsement?
No. The Setu ID does not confer any endorsement, affiliation, or approval from any government body. Use of the Registry name, logo, or National Emblem is strictly prohibited.
How long does verification take?
Typically 3–7 working days after submission of complete documents. You will be notified via email at each stage of the process.
Is there a fee for registration?
Registration on Seva Setu is completely free of charge. No payment is required at any step.
What if my documents are rejected?
You will receive an email explaining the reason for rejection. You may resubmit corrected documents within 30 days without restarting the application process.

About Seva Setu

Seva Setu is a digital initiative designed to simplify the process of identifying and registering Non-Profit Organizations in India. We bridge the gap between civil society and government grant programmes through a single, unified digital platform.

Our Mission

Empowering NPOs with a Unified Identity

India has over 3 million registered non-profit organizations but there is no single, authoritative registry to track them. Seva Setu fills this gap by providing a digital identity — the Setu Unique ID — that any NPO can obtain and use to access government programmes.

Our goal is not to replace existing registration systems but to serve as a layer on top of them — a trusted, verifiable source of identity that government ministries and departments can rely on when processing grant applications.
